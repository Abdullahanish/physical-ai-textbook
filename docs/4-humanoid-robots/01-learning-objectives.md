
# Chapter 4: Learning Objectives

Now we arrive at one of the most exciting and challenging areas of Physical AI. In this chapter, we will focus specifically on robots that are built to look and act like us.

By the end of this chapter, you will be able to:

- **Define a Humanoid Robot:** Explain what a humanoid robot is and why engineers choose to build robots in a human-like form.
- **Understand the "Uncanny Valley":** Describe the Uncanny Valley effect and how it relates to the design of humanoid robots.
- **Identify Key Challenges in Humanoid Robotics:** Name and explain the major difficulties in creating functional humanoid robots, especially bipedal walking and dexterous manipulation.
- **Explain the Zero Moment Point (ZMP):** Grasp the basic concept of the Zero Moment Point and its critical importance for maintaining balance in a walking robot.
- **Recognize the Importance of Dexterous Hands:** Discuss why creating human-like hands is one of the biggest challenges and goals in robotics.
- **Identify Real-World Use Cases:** Brainstorm potential applications where humanoid robots could be uniquely useful.
